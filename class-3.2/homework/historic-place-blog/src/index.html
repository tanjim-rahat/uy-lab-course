Historic Places

  1. Lalbagh Fort

    Lalbagh Fort, situated in the heart of Dhaka, Bangladesh, is a 17th-century Mughal architectural marvel.
    Constructed in 1678 by Prince Muhammad Azam, the son of Emperor Aurangzeb, it remains unfinished
    due to the demise of its principal architect. The fort boasts intricate design elements, including decorative
    motifs, a mosque, a mausoleum, and a Diwan-i-Aam (public audience hall). Its strategic location by
    the Buriganga River made it a significant military fortification during the Mughal era. Today, Lalbagh
    Fort stands as a cherished historical site, drawing visitors for its rich history, stunning architecture, and
    serene ambiance amidst the bustling cityscape of Dhaka.

  2. Mahasthangarh

    Mahasthangarh is an ancient archaeological site in Bogra, Bangladesh, with a history dating back to the
    3rd century BC. It's believed to be the oldest urban archaeological site in Bangladesh and was once the
    capital of the Pundravardhana region. The site spans over 2.5 square kilometers and contains remnants
    of ancient structures, including fortifications, temples, and residential areas. Excavations have revealed
    significant artifacts like coins, sculptures, and pottery, shedding light on the region's ancient civilization
    and trade connections. Mahasthangarh stands as a testament to Bangladesh's rich historical heritage and
    attracts historians, archaeologists, and tourists interested in exploring its ancient past.

    Explore near Mahasthangarh

  3. Ahsan Manzil

    Ahsan Manzil, also known as the Pink Palace, is an architectural gem in Dhaka, Bangladesh, built in the
    late 19th century. It served as the official residence of the Nawabs of Dhaka during the colonial period.
    Its striking pink facade and a blend of Indo-Saracenic and European architectural styles make it a
    significant cultural icon. The palace is situated along the Buriganga River and features a beautiful garden,
    audience halls, a ballroom, and ornate interiors adorned with intricate designs and decorations. Ahsan
    Manzil now operates as a museum, showcasing artifacts, photographs, and relics from the Nawabi era,
    offering visitors a glimpse into Bangladesh's royal history and lifestyle.

    Explore near Ahsan Manzil

  4. Shat Gombuj Mosque

    The Sixty Dome Mosque, locally known as Shat Gombuj Masjid, is a remarkable architectural masterpiece located
    in Bagerhat, Bangladesh. Constructed in the 15th century during the Bengal Sultanate, it's renowned for its unique
    and stunning design featuring sixty pillars that support the mosque's enormous roof. Despite its name, the mosque
    doesn't actually have sixty domes but derives its name from the numerous pillars that create the impression of
    multiple domes from certain angles. The structure showcases exquisite terracotta ornamentation, depicting intricate
    floral and geometric patterns, enhancing its aesthetic beauty. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the mosque stands
    as a testament to the skilled craftsmanship and architectural brilliance of the Bengal Sultanate era, attracting visitors
    and historians alike to marvel at its grandeur and historical significance.

    Explore near Shat Gombuj Mosque

  5. Paharpur Buddhist Monastery

    The Paharpur Buddhist Monastery, also known as Somapura Mahavihara, is an ancient archaeological
    site in Bangladesh, dating back to the 8th century. This UNESCO World Heritage Site stands as a
    testament to the rich Buddhist heritage of the region and served as a renowned center of learning during
    its prime. The monastery's ruins cover a vast area, featuring a central shrine surrounded by 177 cells for
    monks and elaborate terracotta ornamentation, showcasing remarkable architectural craftsmanship.
    Paharpur remains a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists and attracts historians, archaeologists, and
    tourists fascinated by its historical and cultural significance, offering a glimpse into Bangladesh's ancient
    Buddhist civilization.
